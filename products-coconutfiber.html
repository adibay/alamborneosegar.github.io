Coconut Fiber

Coconut fiber is a natural fiber extracted from coconut husk [1] and used in products such as floor mats, mats, brushes and mattresses. Coconut coir is a fibrous material found between the tough inner shell and outer layer of the coconut. Other uses of cocoa coir (made from ripe coconut) are for cushioning, sacks, and for horticulture. White coir, harvested from raw coconuts, is used to make brushes, ropes, ropes and finer fishing nets. Coconut Fiber - Brown is extracted from ripe brown coconuts. It is used for various applications such as the manufacture of Curled Coir Rope which is used in the manufacture of coir mattresses, rubber coir pads, coir cushions, carpet underlay, seat cushions, and to insulate drainage pipes.
